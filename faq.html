Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What are the best activities to do in Sagada?

A: Some popular activities include exploring Sumaguing Cave, visiting the Hanging Coffins, trekking to Bomod-Ok Falls, and watching the sunrise at Kiltepan Viewpoint.

Q: How do I get to Sagada?

A: The easiest way is to travel by bus from Baguio City or Manila. From there, you can take a van or jeepney to Sagada.

Q: What is the best time to visit Sagada?

A: The best time to visit is from November to May, during the dry season when the weather is cooler and clear for outdoor activities.

Q: Are there accommodations in Sagada?

A: Yes, there are a variety of accommodations available, ranging from budget hostels to mid-range inns and lodges. It is advisable to book in advance, especially during peak seasons.

Q: Are local guides required for tours?

A: Yes, hiring a local guide is recommended for activities like caving, trekking, and visiting the Hanging Coffins. Guides ensure your safety and provide information about the places.

Q: Is Sagada safe for solo travelers?

A: Absolutely! Sagada is generally safe for solo travelers. The locals are welcoming and hospitable. However, it’s still best to remain cautious and follow safety guidelines.

Q: Can I use my credit card in Sagada?

A: Most establishments in Sagada only accept cash. It’s best to bring enough cash, as ATMs may not always be available or functional.

Q: Are there food options for vegetarians and vegans?

A: Yes, Sagada offers various vegetarian-friendly meals, with local cafes and restaurants providing fresh vegetables and plant-based dishes.

Q: What cultural practices should I respect while visiting Sagada?

A: Visitors should respect the local customs and traditions. Do not touch the Hanging Coffins, avoid loud noises in sacred areas, and always ask for permission before taking photos of locals or private spaces.

Q: What should I pack when visiting Sagada?

A: Pack comfortable hiking shoes, warm clothing (especially for the cool mornings and evenings), a reusable water bottle, and rain gear if you are visiting during the rainy season.
