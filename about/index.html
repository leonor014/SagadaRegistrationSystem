About Us

Sagada, located in Mountain Province of the Philippines, is a picturesque town renowned for its breathtaking natural landscapes and unique cultural heritage. Nestled in the Cordillera mountain range, Sagada offers visitors stunning views of lush pine forests, dramatic cliffs, and rolling hills that create a serene and refreshing atmosphere. The town is renowned for its extensive limestone cave systems, which include the iconic Sumaguing and Lumiang Caves, where adventurous travelers can explore underground rivers, stalactites, and stalagmites.
One of Sagada’s most distinctive cultural attractions is the tradition of hanging coffins, an ancient burial practice where coffins are suspended on cliffsides, reflecting the community’s deep respect for their ancestors and their unique beliefs about life and death. Beyond its natural and cultural wonders, Sagada is also home to the Igorot people, whose indigenous customs, crafts, and festivals offer visitors an authentic glimpse into their rich heritage.
Sagada is ideal for adventure seekers and nature lovers, offering activities such as trekking to the majestic Bomod-ok Falls, exploring scenic rice terraces, and hiking to viewpoints like Pog-o to witness spectacular sunrises and sea-of-clouds views. The town’s tranquil ambiance, combined with its warm hospitality and vibrant local culture, makes Sagada a perfect destination for those seeking both adventure and cultural immersion in the heart of the Philippine mountains.
